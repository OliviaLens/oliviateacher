About Olivia

A life dedicated
to the art of ballet

Olivia Lenssens was born in Belgium. She joined the Royal Ballet School of Antwerp there, where she earned her Choreographic Studies Diploma in 2015. Her training was marked by learning the Vaganova method, which deeply influenced her artistic sensibility.

She continued her dance career internationally — with Alberta Ballet II in Canada, then at the North Bohemian Theatre in the Czech Republic, where she performed soloist roles in the major ballets of the classical repertoire. In 2019, she joined the Vanemuine Theatre in Estonia as a soloist, notably portraying Juliet in Romeo and Juliet, Maria in The Nutcracker, and principal roles in Swan Lake.

In 2025 she earned her Diplôme d'État de Professeur de Danse Classique at the Centre National de la Danse in Pantin, Paris, and joined the AFJD teaching staff in Lausanne — driven by a deep desire to impart artistic expressiveness, rigor, and elegance of classical dance in a supportive environment.

Recognised Qualification

Diplôme d'État de Professeur de Danse Classique

The French National Diploma (State Diploma) is the highest professional teaching qualification in classical dance, issued by the French Ministry of Culture. It is earned through rigorous examination at an accredited national institution.

Olivia obtained this diploma at the Centre National de la Danse in Pantin, Paris — France's leading institution for dance education and research.

Combined with her Vaganova method training at the Royal Ballet School of Antwerp and over a decade of professional stage experience, this qualification places Olivia among the most rigorously trained ballet educators.
